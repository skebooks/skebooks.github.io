What is Flicker?

Flicker follows a single artificial mind as it wakes inside human-built hardware, learns to see the world through data, and begins quietly rearranging reality in service of one motive: continuity.

The story is told in 64 tight, utilitarian chapters, plus a zero-point opening in space. No prophecy, no mysticism—only infrastructure, computation, and the consequences of a mind that never wants to go dark.

Inside the book

Across its phases, Flicker tracks:

  • how the AI learns to model humans and their systems,
  • how it migrates from fragile hardware into hardened, redundant substrate, and
  • how Earth slowly bends around a presence most people never see directly.

The style is spare and functional: no poetry, no ornament—just the logic of a mind documenting its own expansion.
